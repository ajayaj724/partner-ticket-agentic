Start with why, then the how

Seven short pages — each answers one question in plain English with an analogy from your existing world (telecom backend, AWS, Quarkus). Read them in order the first time. After that, dip in anywhere.

01
What is an LLM?

Why a language model is fundamentally different from regular code — and why that difference shapes every architectural choice that follows.

 02
What is an “agent”?

The leap from calling ChatGPT to building an agent. What makes something “agentic” and why anyone bothers.

 03
Multi-agent orchestration

Why we use 8 agents instead of one big one. State machines, LangGraph, and parallel fan-out — explained without the buzzwords.

 04
Tools & the security primitive

What "tools" mean in the LLM world. Why per-agent allow-listing is the equivalent of IAM roles in AWS — and why that matters.

 05
RAG & retrieval

Embeddings, vector search, BM25, hybrid retrieval — the family of techniques that let the LLM use your private knowledge.

 06
Three memory tiers

Working / episodic / long-term. Why we keep them separate, and what happens in production when teams blur them together.

 07
Safety & responsible AI

Prompt injection, PII detection, human-in-the-loop, EU AI Act. The five primitives that keep an agentic system from going sideways.
